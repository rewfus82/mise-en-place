## What creatine does

Creatine monohydrate is the most effective ergogenic nutritional supplement
available to athletes for increasing high-intensity exercise capacity and lean
body mass during training. Supplementation increases intramuscular
phosphocreatine stores, which improves the capacity to regenerate ATP during
short, intense efforts. Documented benefits include greater strength, power,
sprint performance, and training adaptations.

Creatine monohydrate is the most studied and clinically effective form. Other
forms have not been shown to be superior, and many are less well supported.

## Dosing protocols

A common loading protocol is approximately 0.3 grams per kilogram of body weight
per day, about 20 grams per day split into four doses, for five to seven days,
followed by a maintenance dose of 3 to 5 grams per day. Loading rapidly saturates
muscle creatine stores.

Alternatively, taking 3 to 5 grams per day without a loading phase will saturate
muscle creatine stores over about three to four weeks, with the same end result.
Co-ingesting creatine with carbohydrate or with carbohydrate plus protein can
enhance muscle creatine uptake.

## Safety

There is no compelling scientific evidence that short- or long-term use of creatine
monohydrate causes harm to otherwise healthy individuals, including no evidence of
harm to kidney function in healthy people. Creatine is not a steroid, and the
weight gain seen early in supplementation is primarily water retained within
muscle. It is well tolerated across a wide range of populations.
