## Mechanism

Beta-alanine is the rate-limiting precursor to carnosine, a dipeptide stored in
skeletal muscle. Supplementation reliably increases muscle carnosine content.
Carnosine acts as an intracellular buffer, helping to resist the drop in pH
(rising acidity) that accompanies high-intensity exercise and contributes to
fatigue.

## Dosing

Supplementing with 4 to 6 grams of beta-alanine per day for at least two to four
weeks increases muscle carnosine concentrations. Benefits accrue with continued
use as carnosine stores build. Daily consistency over weeks matters more than
timing around a workout.

A common side effect is paresthesia, a harmless tingling of the skin. Splitting
the daily dose into smaller servings, or using a sustained-release formulation,
reduces this sensation.

## Performance effects

Beta-alanine most clearly improves performance in high-intensity exercise lasting
roughly one to four minutes, where buffering capacity is a limiting factor. It can
improve exercise capacity and reduce fatigue in this duration window. Effects on
very short, primarily strength-based efforts and on long endurance events are
smaller or less consistent.
