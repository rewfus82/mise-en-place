## Meal frequency and body composition

In non-dieting, weight-stable populations, increasing the number of meals per day
does not by itself meaningfully change body composition. The total daily energy
and macronutrient intake matters far more than how many meals it is divided into.
Claims that frequent small meals "stoke the metabolism" are not well supported.

## Athletes and energy restriction

In athletes undergoing energy restriction, eating more frequently may help preserve
lean body mass. Spreading intake across more meals can also improve appetite
control and satiety, which supports adherence to a fat-loss diet.

## Protein distribution

Distributing protein intake across multiple meals through the day, rather than
concentrating it in one or two feedings, better supports muscle protein synthesis.
For this reason, several protein-containing meals spaced across the day are
preferable for athletes seeking to build or retain muscle, even though total
protein remains the primary consideration.
